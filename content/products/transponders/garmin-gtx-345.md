# Garmin GTX 345 Transponder (ADS-B In/Out)

**Manufacturer:** Garmin
**Category:** Transponders & ADS-B
**Price Range:** $6,000–$8,000 (installed)
**STC Coverage:** Universally approved for GA aircraft requiring Part 91.227 ADS-B Out compliance.
**Last Updated:** March 2026

---

## What It Is (The Translation)

The Garmin GTX 345 is the flagship all-in-one general aviation surveillance hub. It is a 1090 MHz Extended Squitter (1090ES) Mode S transponder containing a built-in WAAS GPS receiver and a dual-band ADS-B "In" receiver to overlay free subscription-less weather and traffic data onto panel displays or iPads.

- **For the Entry-Level Tech (Non-Pilot):** Think of it as the airplane's digital license plate and radar detector. By law, modern planes must broadcast their exact GPS location to Air Traffic Control so they don't crash into each other. This box constantly shouts 'Here I am!' to the ground, while simultaneously listening for other airplanes and displaying them on the pilot's screen to avoid mid-air collisions.
- **Why Pilots Want It:** To fly in most controlled airspace, an aircraft is legally required to broadcast its ADS-B "Out" position. The GTX 345 fulfills that legal mandate while simultaneously supplying the pilot with stunning, free in-flight weather radar and real-time traffic alerts on their iPad or Garmin GPS navigator. 

## How It Works

The GTX 345 performs two distinct, highly critical functions simultaneously:

- **ADS-B "Out" (The Legal Mandate):** Because the 345 models typically include a built-in WAAS GPS source, they constantly determine their precise position in space. The transponder continuously "squits" (transmits) a digital packet containing the aircraft's ID, vector, and 3D position out to the FAA's ground-based radar network and to other aircraft.
- **ADS-B "In" (The Pilot's Benefit):** The unit contains a dual-band receiver (listening on both 978 MHz and 1090 MHz) to detect the squits of every other ADS-B equipped aircraft within dozens of miles. Furthermore, it receives the FIS-B (Flight Information Services-Broadcast) datalink broadcast from FAA ground towers, pulling down free regional NEXRAD weather radar images, METARs, and TFRs.

## Installation Considerations

- **Consolidation:** The GTX 345 is designed precisely to fit the massive 1.65-inch-tall radio stack slot historically occupied by the universally ubiquitous BendixKing KT 76A or Garmin GTX 327 transponder. 
- **The Internal GPS WAAS Advantage:** If the GTX 345 is ordered with the internal WAAS GPS option, the avionics shop simply needs to mount a low-cost, small GPS puck antenna on the upper fuselage. This completely severs the transponder from needing to interface with the aircraft's primary panel GPS (like a GTN 750), shielding the transponder from the notoriously expensive labor of hooking the boxes together via complex ARINC 429 digital databuses.
- **Connectivity:** The GTX 345 features Connext Bluetooth integration. It wirelessly broadcasts the traffic and weather data to up to two iPads running ForeFlight or Garmin Pilot simultaneously.

## Objective Performance & Specs

- **Transponder Output:** 1090 MHz Mode S Extended Squitter (1090ES). Highly critical because unlike 978 UAT transponders, 1090ES natively satisfies the requirements for operating in Class A airspace (above 18,000 feet) and complies with international regulations spanning Canada, Mexico, and Europe.
- **Receiver Input:** Dual-band ADS-B In (receives 978 UAT for weather/general aviation traffic, and 1090ES for airliner/high-altitude traffic).
- **Audio Output:** Interfaces with the aircraft audio panel to aggressively broadcast audible "Traffic! Twelve O'clock, High, Two Miles!" warnings. 
- **Attitude Sensor:** Includes an internal solid-state attitude sensor (AHRS) dedicated purely to providing backup pitch-and-roll information directly to the pilot's iPad.

## Related Products

- **Appareo Stratus ESG:** 1090ES ADS-B Out transponder without ADS-B In. Requires a separate portable Stratus receiver for traffic/weather display.
- **Garmin GTX 335:** Same transponder architecture without the ADS-B In receivers and Bluetooth module. Lower cost, provides identical ADS-B Out compliance.
- **L3Harris Lynx NGT-9000:** 1090ES transponder with a built-in color touchscreen for self-contained traffic and weather visualization without external displays.

## Key Technical Facts

- 1090ES Mode S transponder with dual-band ADS-B In (978 MHz + 1090 MHz). Satisfies 14 CFR 91.225/91.227.
- Internal WAAS GPS receiver option eliminates dependency on panel navigator for position data.
- Bluetooth Connext broadcasts traffic and weather wirelessly to up to two tablets running compatible EFB applications.
- Internal AHRS provides backup pitch-and-roll attitude data to compatible iPad applications.
- FIS-B data includes NEXRAD weather, METARs, TAFs, and TFRs — subscription-free.
- 1090ES compliance covers Class A airspace, Canadian airspace, and international operations where 978 UAT is not accepted.
