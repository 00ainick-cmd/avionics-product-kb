# Avidyne SkyTrax 3000
**Manufacturer:** Avidyne
**Price Range:** OEM / High-End Corporate Retrofit

Announced in late 2025, the SkyTrax 3000 is a dual-band ADS-B In system aimed squarely at the Part 25 corporate jet market (initially targeting Hawker 800XP/850XP/900XP series). 

While GA technicians are used to installing simple ADS-B In receivers (like a GTX 345 or a Stratus), integrating ADS-B In onto existing, legacy TCAS II (Traffic Collision Avoidance System) architecture in heavy jets is a notoriously expensive and complex nightmare. The SkyTrax 3000 is Avidyne's "silver bullet" solution to this problem.

## The Problem It Solves

Many corporate jets are equipped with older TCAS II systems and legacy flight displays (like Collins Pro Line 21). Upgrading these aircraft to display modern ADS-B In traffic (which shows exact GPS positions rather than just relative bearing/range) and FIS-B weather typically requires ripping out the old TCAS computers and replacing the main flight displays at an astronomical cost.

## The SkyTrax 3000 Solution

The SkyTrax 3000 acts as an intelligent "middleman." It is a TSO-certified system designed to inject modern ADS-B In capabilities *without* requiring modifications to the existing TCAS II avionics or the primary cockpit displays.

1.  **Dual-Band Reception:** It receives both 1090 MHz (global standard) and 978 MHz UAT (US weather/traffic standard) ADS-B signals.
2.  **Data Blending:** It takes the highly accurate ADS-B traffic data, TIS-B (Traffic Information Service-Broadcast) data from ATC, and standard TCAS II targets, and blends them into a single, cohesive traffic picture.
3.  **Seamless Injection:** It outputs this blended data in a format that the legacy flight displays can seamlessly read and display. The pilots see a vastly improved traffic picture (with directional vectors and flight IDs) on their existing screens.
4.  **Weather:** It also receives FIS-B weather (NEXRAD, METARs, TAFs) and displays it on compatible MFDs or through Wi-Fi to an iPad running an EFB application.

## Installation Realities

For the technician, the SkyTrax 3000 is a major labor-saver on large airframes.

- **No Display Swaps:** You avoid the downtime and risk of removing heavy CRT or early LCD flight displays.
- **Antenna Placement:** Installing dual-band ADS-B antennas on pressurized, high-altitude aircraft requires specialized knowledge of pressure vessel penetration and structural doublers, but it is vastly simpler than a full panel tear-down.
- **Interfacing:** The technician must precisely configure the ARINC 429 output data buses to match the specific "language" expected by the legacy displays.

## The Bottom Line

The SkyTrax 3000 is a highly specialized piece of equipment. It proves that Avidyne is expanding its reach beyond GA and light jets, creating clever, bolt-on solutions that save corporate operators hundreds of thousands of dollars in mandated upgrade costs.
