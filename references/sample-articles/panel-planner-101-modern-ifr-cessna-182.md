# Panel Planner 101: Modern IFR Cessna 182

**Source:** Aviation Consumer
**Author:** Larry Anglisano
**Date:** January 2024
**URL:** https://aviationconsumer.com/avionics/panel-planner-101-modern-ifr-cessna-182/
**Category:** Upgrade Planning / GPS-Nav / EFIS
**Products covered:** Garmin GTN 650Xi, GTN 750Xi, GI 275, GNS 430W, GMX 200, S-TEC 30, Trig TX56A, Apollo SL30, Apollo SL15M

---

This Panel Planner examines a 1976 Cessna 182P with approximately 100 annual IFR flight hours. While the current avionics setup supports IFR operations, some equipment approaches obsolescence and the upgrade strategy balances IFR redundancy, supportability, and limited installation downtime.

## Current Panel Assessment

Audio system: Apollo SL15M audio panel with integrated intercom (legacy PS Engineering 7000 series) — retain due to continued support. Navigation: GMX 200 MFD (aging; repair costs ~$1,335 at Garmin flat rates), GNS 430W (discontinued support announced by Garmin), and Apollo SL30 secondary navcomm. Transponder: Garmin GTX 345 handles ADS-B Out — no upgrade needed. Autopilot: S-TEC 30 rate-based system serving dual role as turn coordinator — retain.

## Option 1: Conservative Retrofit (~$30,000)

Replace GNS 430W with GTN 650Xi ($13,595). Retain GMX 200 MFD and existing CDI. Requires rewiring; GNS 430W estimated resale value ~$5,000. Add Trig TX56A navcomm ($4,500) as secondary replacement for Apollo SL30. Install two Garmin GI 275 instruments — one as AI, one as EHSI (~$9,000).

## Option 2: Larger Screen Upgrade

Remove both GMX 200 and GNS 430W. Install GTN 750Xi MFD ($19,695). Comparable secondary navcomm and glass instrument additions.

## Key Priorities

Preference for Garmin equipment to leverage existing operational knowledge. Prioritizing familiarity with manufacturer interface, support longevity, and avoiding excessive installation downtime rather than pursuing flagship autopilots or cutting-edge configurations.
