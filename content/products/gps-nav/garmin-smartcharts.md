# Garmin SmartCharts
**Manufacturer:** Garmin
**Price Range:** Subscription-Based Feature in PlaneSync/Connext Portals

Launched in May 2025, SmartCharts represents a fundamental shift in how pilots interact with terminal procedures (instrument approaches, Standard Terminal Arrivals, and Departure procedures). It moves away from static "paper on glass" PDFs toward dynamic, data-driven mapping.

For avionics technicians, understanding SmartCharts is important when demonstrating new TXi, G3000/G5000 PRIME, or GTN Xi systems to customers, as it is a major selling point for upgrading. 

## The Shift From Static to Dynamic

Historically, when a pilot loaded an approach plate on an MFD (Multi-Function Display), the system simply displayed a georeferenced PDF image of the FAA approach plate. It was static, often cluttered, and scaled poorly when zoomed in.

SmartCharts rebuilds the terminal procedure using raw navigational data.

- **De-cluttering:** Because the chart is built from layered data, the pilot can selectively remove irrelevant information. For instance, if an approach has four different IAFs (Initial Approach Fixes) and the pilot is only cleared to one, they can filter out the lines and text for the other three, significantly cleaning up the display.
- **Seamless Integration:** SmartCharts seamlessly overlay onto the primary enroute moving map. There is no more switching back and forth between the "Map" page and the "Charts" page. The approach plan simply becomes an extension of the enroute flight plan on the map.
- **Dynamic Scaling:** When zooming in, the text and symbols remain crisp, upright, and legible, unlike a PDF which simply magnifies blurry pixels.
- **Graphical NOTAMs:** As of late 2025, SmartCharts includes an overlay for Graphical NOTAMs (Notices to Air Missions), visually alerting pilots to closed runways or taxiways directly on the airport diagram.

## Installation & Data Realities

### The Subscription Model
SmartCharts are not a hardware upgrade; they are a data subscription upgrade. They require a compatible, modern Garmin display (like the GTN Xi series or TXi displays running current software) and an active Garmin Pilot or PlaneSync database subscription that includes the SmartCharts tier.

### Technician Considerations
- **Software Updates:** Ensure the customer's GTN Xi or TXi displays are updated to the latest main software cadence. Older software versions will not support the dynamic rendering engine required for SmartCharts.
- **Database Management:** SmartCharts rely on massive amounts of highly detailed navigation data. If a customer is experiencing slow rendering times or missing procedures, the first troubleshooting step is ensuring their databases are fully updated and uncorrupted, often managed seamlessly via the Garmin PlaneSync GDL 60 datalink.

## The Bottom Line

SmartCharts take the mental gymnastics out of interpreting complex terminal procedures. By turning static paper into a dynamic, filterable, and seamless map overlay, Garmin has significantly improved situational awareness during the highest-workload phases of flight.
