# Transponder Upgrades: Driven By ADS-B

**Source:** Aviation Consumer
**Author:** Larry Anglisano
**Date:** June 2015
**URL:** https://aviationconsumer.com/avionics/transponder-upgrades-driven-by-ads-b/
**Category:** Transponders / ADS-B
**Products covered:** Sandia STX165, BendixKing KT76C, Garmin GTX327, GTX330ES, GTX33, Avidyne AXP340, Trig TT series, L-3 Lynx NGT9000, Appareo Stratus ESG, Becker AR

---

Aircraft owners facing the ADS-B mandate need to carefully evaluate transponder upgrades. This article examines next-generation multifunction transponders that consolidate ADS-B and traditional transponder functions into single units.

## Basic Mode A/C Transponders

The Sandia STX165 ($1,850) fits standard instrument cutouts and includes an integral Mode C altitude encoder certified to 35,000 feet. The encoder connects directly to the aircraft's static system, eliminating aging static lines.

BendixKing's KT76C is a digital model compatible with existing KT76A wiring and mounting. Despite digital features, it retains a cavity tube for power output, which generates significant heat and eventually fails.

Garmin's GTX327 has a short chassis design that suits panels with limited clearance. The remote GTX32 version can be controlled via GTN750 touchscreen.

## 1090ES ADS-B Transponders

Garmin GTX330ES/GTX33 are compatible with Garmin's GNS530W/430W and GTN-series navigators, plus Avidyne's IFD540/440. Cross-brand compatibility remains vulnerable to future software updates.

Avidyne's AXP340 is approved to work with IFD540/440 and GNS530W/430W navigators, though incompatible with GTN750/650 systems.

Trig Avionics offers space-saving models compatible with Garmin GNS navigators but requiring additional wiring for GPS data integration.

L-3 Aviation Lynx NGT9000 is a comprehensive solution with internal WAAS GPS, dual-band ADS-B receiver, and color touchscreen display. Configurations range from $8,300 to $13,384.

## Key Recommendations

For basic Mode A/C needs, the Sandia STX165 offers strong value. For 1090ES upgrades, existing WAAS navigator choice significantly influences the transponder decision. Garmin designs products to work primarily in its own architecture.

For GTN users, the GTX330ES is the top choice. For Avidyne IFD540 owners, Avidyne's AXP340 ensures optimal compatibility. Self-contained transponders with integrated WAAS GPS provide independence from future software conflicts.
