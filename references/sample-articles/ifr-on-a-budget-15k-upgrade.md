# IFR On A Budget: The $15K Upgrade

**Source:** Aviation Consumer
**Author:** Larry Anglisano
**Date:** April 2020
**URL:** https://aviationconsumer.com/avionics/ifr-on-a-budget-the-15k-upgrade/
**Category:** Upgrade Planning / EFIS / GPS-Nav
**Products covered:** Garmin G5, GI 275, GPS 175, GNC 355, GNX 375, Aspen E5, uAvionix AV-30-C

---

This guide addresses equipping aircraft for instrument flight training and basic IFR operations without excessive spending. Modern entry-level avionics have made legal IFR capability more affordable than ever, though the crowded marketplace requires careful planning to avoid overspending.

## Small-Screen EFIS Systems

The Aspen Evolution E5 ($4,995) offers budget-conscious pilots an alternative. This model trickles down from the flagship Evolution Max while maintaining improved display technology. It requires a remote sensor module installation but eliminates the vacuum system, saving approximately 15 pounds.

The Garmin G5 provides dual-instrument capability — an attitude indicator and digital heading indicator — around $7,000 total. It features four-hour backup batteries and integrates with various navigators including legacy Garmin units.

Garmin's GI 275 series offers more sophisticated functionality, with base models starting at $3,195 and ADAHRS versions at $3,995. These instruments provide synthetic vision options, engine monitoring, and superior display capabilities compared to the G5. The GI 275 is roughly $1,500 more than the G5 but simply does more.

The uAvionix AV-30-C ($2,299) features a 3-inch color display, built-in angle of attack, audio/altitude alerting, and fits 3.25-inch cutouts. Recent upgrades include IFR compatibility via AV-HSI smart adapter and AV-Link traffic streaming ($299).

## Navigator Options

Budget GPS solutions include the Garmin GPS 175 ($4,995), GNC 355 ($6,995), and GNX 375 ($7,995). Each sacrifices certain capabilities — primarily ground-based VHF navigation — to keep costs manageable.

## Realistic Budget Breakdown

A practical $15,000 configuration might include dual Garmin G5 instruments ($4,900) paired with a GNC 355 navigator ($6,995), leaving approximately $3,105 for installation and contingencies. Alternative configurations using GI 275 instruments cost roughly $3,500 more than comparable G5 setups.

## Installation Considerations

Consult directly with avionics shops before purchasing used equipment, as many configurations require specific installation kits, mounting hardware, and airworthiness documentation (FAA Form 8130-3).
