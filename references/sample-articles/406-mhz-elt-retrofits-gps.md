This article examines GPS-enabled 406 MHz Emergency Locator Transmitters for aircraft retrofits. While the FAA requires installed ELTs, it hasn't mandated the newer 406 MHz standard. Modern 406 MHz beacons transmit 5-watt signal bursts every 50 seconds with encrypted short-message data, providing accuracy as tight as one mile compared to approximately 15 miles for older systems.

## GPS Integration

GPS interfaces maximize search effectiveness by transmitting location coordinates alongside programmed address data. Some systems connect external panel GPS units via serial databus rather than incorporating internal receivers. This distinction significantly impacts installation complexity and labor costs. Installation during annual inspections is recommended.

## Premium Internal GPS Options

The ELT406GPS ($1,600) features an internal GPS receiver with low-profile blade antenna housing both GPS and 406 MHz elements. Uses three replaceable D-cell lithium batteries (~$200).

The Kannad AF Integra ($1,200) includes built-in GPS with internal antenna suitable for fiberglass and fabric aircraft. Six-year battery life ($200 replacement).

The Skyhunter 406 ($1,150 with whip; $1,500 with blade) offers internal GPS with USB programming capability.

## Budget-Friendly Alternatives

The Artex ELT-1000 ($620) lacks internal GPS but provides external GPS compatibility. Flexibility with antenna options and Garmin fleet preference makes it popular among shops.

The Artex ELT-345 ($550) limits antenna selection to whip-only and aircraft under 225 knots.

The ACK E-04/R ($450) provides upgrade paths for owners with existing ACK models.

## Installation Considerations

All 406 MHz beacons require panel-mounted remote control switches for manual activation and self-testing. Mounting points must withstand 100G loads. Faster airframes may need higher-speed-rated antennas, with pressurized aircraft potentially requiring additional approvals.

## Recommendations

The Artex ELT-1000 is favored for fair pricing and antenna flexibility. For integrated GPS capability, the Kannad Integra represents good value at $1,200. Installation costs typically hover around $3,000, though coordination with annual inspections can reduce labor expenses.
