# Choosing Retrofit Glass: Aspen Takes the Prize

**Source:** Aviation Consumer
**Author:** Larry Anglisano
**Date:** May 2011
**URL:** https://aviationconsumer.com/industry-news/choosing-retrofit-glass-aspen-takes-the-prize/
**Category:** EFIS/PFD / Flight Displays / Comparison
**Products covered:** Garmin G500, Aspen EFD1000 Evolution, Evolution 1500, Evolution 2000, GAD43, EA100

---

When pilots consider major avionics upgrades, two systems dominate the aftermarket: Garmin's G500 and Aspen's EFD1000-series. While the larger G500 initially attracts buyers, installation complexity often makes Aspen the winning choice.

## Display Specifications

Garmin G500 features dual screens (PFD and MFD) in one bezel, each measuring 6.5 inches with 640x480 VGA resolution and 65,536 colors. The system weighs approximately 15 pounds with extensive wiring bundles.

Aspen EFD1000 has a single 760x400 LCD screen measuring six inches with 32,768 colors and three-pound weight. Optional second or third screens expand capabilities incrementally.

## Integration Advantages

Aspen integrated flight-critical electronics within the display itself, including the AHRS and digital air data computer, saving installation space and effort. The G500 requires multiple remote units: GRS77 AHRS, GDC74A air data computer, temperature probe, and GMU44 magnetometer.

On radio compatibility, Aspen's remote analog converter (ACU) accepts vintage nav radios, while the G500 only accepts input from Garmin's GNS-series and SL30 Nav. Older KX155 radios won't integrate with Garmin systems.

## Autopilot Integration

Both systems support digital autopilot emulators. Garmin's GAD43 and Aspen's EA100 successfully replace older gyro systems. The G500 can command the S-TEC with a $2,995 enablement card. The Aspen cannot.

## Redundancy

A dual-Aspen setup (Evolution 2000) enables removal of backup flight instruments except the attitude gyro, offering dual-AHRS redundancy. Aspen's Achilles heel is pitot-static blockage — loss of either pitot or static data will remove heading and attitude on all Evolution displays and disable the autopilot.

## Pricing

Basic G500: $15,995. G500 with SVT and GAD43: $23,985. Evolution 1500 (Aspen): $14,990. Evolution 2000 with EA100, battery, synthetic vision: $22,780. Installation labor typically favors Aspen, further narrowing the price gap.

## Conclusion

Neither solution suits all cockpits. The G500 excels in feature set and screen real estate, while Aspen offers superior installation practicality and radio flexibility for pilots with legacy equipment and budget constraints. Expect a significant learning curve with either system.
