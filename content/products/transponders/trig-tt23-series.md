# Trig TT23 Compact Transponder Series
**Manufacturer:** Trig Avionics (A Dynon Group Company)
**Price Range:** $2,500 - $3,500 (depending on GPS inclusion and model)

Launched in November 2025 following Trig's merger with the Dynon Group, the TT23 Series represents the next generation of Trig's highly successful compact transponder line. It directly replaces the aging TT21 and TT22 models, offering a smaller footprint, less weight, and modern ADS-B Out capabilities for space-constrained panels.

For the avionics technician working on Light Sport Aircraft (LSA), experimentals, gliders, and tight certified panels (like tandem-seat taildraggers), the TT23 is a foundational product.

## System Architecture

The TT23 series utilizes a unique two-piece architecture designed to save panel space:

1.  **The Control Head:** A tiny, circular control head (often fitting a standard 2.25-inch instrument hole or utilizing a custom compact rectangular bezel) sits on the panel. It features a new, bright LCD screen and intuitive push-button controls.
2.  **The Remote Hardware:** The actual transponder transmitter/receiver "box" is mounted remotely behind the panel or avionics bay, keeping heat and bulk away from the pilot's instruments.

## Two Flavors: TT23 vs. TT23G

Trig offers the unit in two distinct configurations to accommodate different aircraft architectures:

- **The Standard TT23:** This model requires an external, certified WAAS GPS position source (like a Garmin GTN or Avidyne IFD navigator) to satisfy the FAA's ADS-B Out mandate. It takes the GPS single via an RS-232 data line.
- **The TT23G (Built-in GPS):** This is the stand-out model. It features a built-in WAAS GPS receiver. It is primarily targeted at experimental aircraft, LSAs, and VFR-only aircraft that do not have a certified panel-mount navigator. By adding a simple GPS antenna, the TT23G acts as an "all-in-one" mandate compliance box.

## Key Advancements over the TT22

- **Size and Weight:** The new remote unit is up to 15% lighter and smaller than its predecessor, a critical factor for weight-sensitive LSAs and gliders.
- **Built-in Altitude Encoder:** Older installations required a separate, bulky altitude encoder wired to the transponder. The TT23 internalizes this, drastically simplifying the wiring harness and reducing points of failure.
- **Modern LCD:** The control head features a crisper layout and better viewing angles.

## Installation Realities

### The Technician's Dream
The TT23 is beloved by technicians for its simplicity. With the built-in altitude encoder (and the built-in GPS on the 'G' model), the wiring harness is reduced to power, ground, an antenna connection, and a few optional discrete switches (like a squat switch). 

### Configuration Notes
Setting up the TT23 requires entering the aircraft's ICAO hex code and VFR squawk defaults via the control head's menu system. It is straightforward and does not require a laptop to configure.

### Dynon Integration
Given Trig's merger with Dynon, expect tight integration via serial data with the Dynon SkyView HDX systems, allowing pilots to control the remote TT23 transponder directly from the large glass displays without needing the dedicated control head on the panel.

## The Bottom Line

The Trig TT23 series is the quintessential "problem solver" for small aircraft. It provides robust, Class 1 Mode S / ADS-B Out capabilities without eating up precious panel real estate or payload capacity.
