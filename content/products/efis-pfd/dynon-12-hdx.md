# Dynon 12-inch SkyView HDX Display
**Manufacturer:** Dynon Avionics
**Price Range:** $5,500 - $6,500+ (Display Only)

Announced at Sun 'n Fun 2024, the Dynon 12-inch SkyView HDX is the largest and brightest display ever offered by the experimental-heavyweight manufacturer. Designed to push the boundaries of "big glass" in experimental amateur-built (E-AB) and light sport aircraft (LSA) panels, this massive screen offers a significant upgrade in situational awareness.

For avionics technicians building custom panels for the experimental market, the 12" HDX fundamentally shifts how panel layout is planned.

## Expanding the View

The primary draw of the 12-inch HDX is its sheer physical size, dwarfing the previous 10-inch flagship model.

- **Brighter and Clearer:** The new display features significantly improved brightness nits and advanced anti-glare coatings, making it incredibly readable in direct sunlight—a crucial factor for low-wing bubble-canopy aircraft like RVs or Glasairs.
- **Split-Screen Dominance:** While a 10-inch screen can feel cramped when split between a Primary Flight Display (PFD) and a moving map/Engine Indication System (EIS), the 12-inch screen handles tri-pane layouts effortlessly. The pilot can view a large PFD, a detailed map, and full engine parameters without sacrificing critical data readability.
- **Wider Viewing Angles:** Improved LCD technology ensures that the display remains perfectly readable even when viewed from an acute angle (e.g., from the passenger seat or an offset pilot position).

## Installation Realities

### The "Glass" Footprint
The most significant impact on the technician is the panel footprint. 

- **Custom Cutting:** A 12-inch screen will dominate the panel of a typical two-seat experimental. It often requires custom-cutting the entire aluminum fascia to perfectly center the massive display while finding room for backup instruments and radios.
- **Depth Clearance:** Larger screens often have slightly larger cooling fin profiles on the rear. Technicians must ensure adequate clearance behind the panel to prevent interference with control column pushrods or wiring bundles.
- **Power Draw:** Generating that much brightness requires power. The electrical bus must be designed or upgraded to handle the higher amperage draw of the massive screen, especially true if an owner is installing *dual* 12-inch screens.

## Seamless Integration

The brilliant part of Dynon's architecture is that the "brains" of the system remain the same. The 12-inch display is fundamentally just a very large, interactive monitor. 

It utilizes the exact same network components (ADAHRS, Engine Monitoring Modules, Autopilot Servos) as the smaller 10-inch or 7-inch HDX screens. This means an existing Dynon customer can pull out a 10-inch screen, cut a larger hole, plug in the 12-inch screen with the same network cables, and significantly bump up their visual real estate without having to rewire the entire airplane's sensor network.

## The Bottom Line

The Dynon 12-inch SkyView HDX brings high-end corporate jet "wow factor" displays into the experimental market. It demands careful panel planning from the technician but rewards the owner with unparalleled visual clarity and split-screen usability.
