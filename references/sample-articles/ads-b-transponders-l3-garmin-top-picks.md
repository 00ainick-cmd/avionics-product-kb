# ADS-B Transponders: L-3, Garmin Top Picks

**Source:** Aviation Consumer
**Author:** Larry Anglisano
**Date:** October 2016
**URL:** https://aviationconsumer.com/aircraftreviews/software/ads-b-transponders-l-3-garmin-top-picks/
**Category:** Transponders / ADS-B
**Products covered:** Garmin GTX 335, GTX 345, L-3 NGT-9000, Appareo Stratus ESG, Avidyne AXP340, BendixKing KT74, Trig TT31, FreeFlight

---

This comprehensive guide examines ADS-B compliant transponders for aircraft equipage before the mandate deadline. A new breed of multifunction transponders is making equipage easier than ever, though the market presents numerous options requiring careful consideration.

## Appareo

The Stratus ESG ADS-B transponder lists at $2,995 standalone ($3,495 with 2i receiver). It features internal WAAS GPS, designed for federated avionics. The remote receiver option (2i) enables wireless tablet connectivity via ForeFlight.

## Avidyne

The AXP340 transponder is compatible with IFD navigators and Garmin GNS/GTN series. It requires a separate MLB100 receiver ($2,500) for ADS-B In capability. A remote AXP322 version is available at $5,450.

## BendixKing/Trig/FreeFlight

The KT74 1090ES transponder works with Garmin WAAS navigators. Trig TT31 and TT22/21 models are available. The NexNav remote GPS receiver option runs $1,100.

## Garmin

The GTX335 is the entry-level model at $2,995 ($3,795 with internal WAAS). The GTX345 features ADS-B In receiver at $4,995 ($5,795 with internal WAAS). Both include built-in Bluetooth for tablet connectivity and are compatible with Garmin Pilot and ForeFlight applications.

## L-3 NGT-9000

This multifunction ADS-B system with built-in WAAS GPS and dual-band receiver lists starting at $5,490. It features dual touchscreens for weather and traffic display, with Wi-Fi module available for tablet integration. Optional eTAWS terrain awareness adds $4,000.

## Installation Considerations

Installation complexity varies significantly. A typical NGT-9000 installation requires approximately 15 hours labor (at $110/hour = $1,650), resulting in total costs around $7,350. Comparable GTX345 installation takes roughly 13 hours, yielding approximately $6,630 all-in.

Key decision factors include mission profile and altitude requirements, existing avionics compatibility, display preferences, and budget constraints.

## Top Picks

The L-3 NGT-9000 and Garmin GTX345 are equally favored for their rich feature sets and growth potential. For budget-conscious buyers with ForeFlight preference, the Stratus ESGi system is recommended as offering the cheapest transponder-based ADS-B In/Out system available.

No single solution fits all applications.
