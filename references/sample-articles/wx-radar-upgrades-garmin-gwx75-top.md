This guide examines weather radar repair and upgrade options for general aviation aircraft, with emphasis on Garmin's latest GWX series systems. It addresses the practical decision between repairing legacy radar systems or investing in modern replacements.

## Repair vs. Replace Economics

Most avionics shops outsource radar repairs due to a shortage of experienced technicians. Duncan Avionics and Mid-Continent Instruments receive high marks for supporting BendixKing RDR2000 series radars commonly found in light twins and high-performance singles.

Mid-Continent quotes approximately $5,490 for an overhaul exchange on RDR2000 systems, though costs escalate significantly with magnetron failures or circuit board issues. A new outright ART2000 unit carries a list price around $22,178.

## Garmin GWX Series Evolution

The GWX line descended from Narco Avionics technology acquired by Garmin in 2000. The progression moved from magnetron-based systems to solid-state architecture:

- GWX 68: Initial 6,500-watt magnetron system
- GWX 70: Solid-state successor using 40-watt transistors with superior reliability
- GWX 75: Next-generation model priced at $21,995, offering increased processing power and expanded scanning

## Solid-State Advantages

Solid-state radar technology provides benefits over magnetron designs. While transmit power decreased dramatically, pulse energy extends to almost 500 microseconds, combined with digital signal processing for superior resolution and range.

The GWX 70 and 75 support Doppler capabilities including ground clutter suppression and turbulence detection, reducing pilot workload compared to legacy systems.

## Display Compatibility

Garmin's GWX radars connect via Ethernet to most current retrofit displays, including TXi-series MFD/PFD units, GTN 750 navigators, and G500/600 systems. Older displays like the GMX 200 show compatibility but with reduced functionality.

## Antenna Considerations

Antenna size directly impacts performance through beam width. A 10-inch antenna typical of wing-mounted pods produces a 10-degree beam, whereas airline-grade 30-inch dishes generate 3-degree beams. Available radome sizes include 10-inch, 12-inch, and 18-inch models.

## Training Recommendation

Garmin's Aviation Weather Radar 2.0 eLearning course at $149 is recommended, emphasizing that radar interpretation requires skill development, particularly regarding shadow effects indicating tall storm cells.

## Caveat for Buyers

Prospective purchasers of radar-equipped aircraft should request maintenance logs and have systems tested before purchase, as non-functional equipment sometimes remains installed.
