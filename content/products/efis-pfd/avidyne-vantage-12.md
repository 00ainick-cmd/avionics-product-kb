# Avidyne Vantage 12 Flight Display System
**Manufacturer:** Avidyne
**Price Range:** High (Corporate / High-End GA)

Certified in July 2025 initially for thousands of Entegra-equipped Cirrus SR20 and SR22 aircraft, the Vantage 12 represents Avidyne's flagship retrofit display system. It is designed to replace aging first-generation glass cockpits (specifically the Avidyne Entegra EX5000 displays) with massive, modern, 12-inch LCD touchscreens.

For technicians working in Cirrus Service Centers or high-end MROs, the Vantage 12 is a critical upgrade path that saves significant installation time compared to transitioning a legacy airframe to a competitor's system.

## The "Plug and Play" Philosophy

The primary selling point of the Vantage 12 system to both owners and installation shops is its retrofit simplicity.

- **EZ Installation Kits:** Avidyne released specialized "EZ Installation Kits" that include pre-manufactured adapter harnesses. 
- **Retaining Existing Avionics:** The Vantage 12 is designed to talk directly to the existing Avidyne DFC90 autopilot and interface seamlessly with existing remote sensors (like DAUs - Data Acquisition Units). 
- **The Competitor Comparison:** Upgrading an Entegra Cirrus to a Garmin G500 TXi or G3X Touch system requires ripping out almost every piece of wire and sensor in the airplane and installing a new autopilot. The Vantage 12 drastically cuts labor costs by reusing the existing infrastructure.

## Key Features

1.  **Dual 12-inch Displays:** The system typically consists of two massive 12-inch touchscreen displays acting as a PFD and MFD. They offer incredibly bright, high-resolution graphics.
2.  **3D Synthetic Vision:** The Vantage provides a full, edge-to-edge 3D synthetic vision environment on the PFD, drastically improving spatial awareness in IMC (Instrument Meteorological Conditions).
3.  **Dual ARS (Attitude Reference Systems):** The system features dual, redundant attitude sensors, significantly improving reliability over the single, mechanical gyros found in older systems.
4.  **Hybrid Touch Interface:** Pilots can interact with the system entirely via touch, or utilize dedicated knobs and buttons along the bezel—a crucial feature for data entry in turbulence.

## Technician Talking Points

- **Configuration:** While the wiring harness is simplified via adapters, the technician must still be adept at software configuration, ensuring the new displays are correctly talking to the legacy DAU for engine indications and the IFD navigators mapped correctly.
- **Panel Work:** While the wiring is easier, the physical displays are large. Technicians must carefully modify or order new custom-cut metal panels to accommodate the massive 12-inch screens.
- **The "IFD" Synergy:** To get the most out of the Vantage 12, it is almost entirely paired with dual Avidyne IFD navigators (like the IFD550 or IFD440), creating a completely integrated, bezel-to-bezel Avidyne ecosystem.

## The Bottom Line

The Vantage 12 is Avidyne's masterstroke for customer retention. By creating a visually stunning, massive 12-inch display system that is vastly easier (and cheaper) to install than a full Garmin swap, they have solidified their hold on the aging Entegra Cirrus fleet.
