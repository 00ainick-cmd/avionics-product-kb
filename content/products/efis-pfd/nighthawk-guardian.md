# Nighthawk Flight Systems Guardian
**Manufacturer:** Nighthawk Flight Systems
**Price Range:** $40,000 (Basic) - $110,000+ (Fully Integrated 4-Display System)

Profiled in the September 2025 issue of Avionics News, the Nighthawk Guardian represents a completely novel approach to integrated flight deck architecture for Part 23 Class I/II and experimental aircraft. 

While manufacturers like Garmin and Avidyne rely on robust (and heavy) aluminum racking systems behind the panel to hold their remote Line Replaceable Units (LRUs), Nighthawk has fundamentally rethought how avionics computers are physically mounted and networked, creating a massive weight-saving benefit.

## The "NEST" Architecture

The defining feature of the Guardian system is the NEST (National Electronics System Tray).

- **Weight Savings:** The NEST is a centralized hub made of advanced composites rather than heavy metal. It is designed to rack the system's LRUs while being up to 50% lighter than traditional integrated systems. A NEST containing three standard LRUs weighs under 5 pounds.
- **Remote Mounting:** The NEST does not need to be directly behind the instrument panel. It can be mounted anywhere within 25 feet of the primary displays, allowing installers to push the CG (Center of Gravity) aft or utilize dead space in the tailcone.
- **The "Blade Link" System:** Perhaps the biggest innovation for the technician in the field is how the LRUs connect to the NEST. Nighthawk utilizes "blade links"—simple, robust plastic jumpers—that allow for tool-less, rapid swap-outs of LRUs on the flight line without wrestling with massive, multi-pin D-sub connectors in blind spots.

## Display Options

The Guardian system supports up to four networked displays.

- **Size Options:** Displays are available in a massive 12-inch (providing 11.6 inches of usable screen area) and a smaller 7-inch touch configuration for tighter panels or standby usage.
- **Mission Control Unit (MCU):** The primary processing happens in the MCU module slotted into the NEST. A single MCU can drive two displays. For a full four-display setup, a second MCU is added to the NEST.

## Installation & Interfacing Realities

- **Autopilot Compatibility:** A major selling point for the retrofit market is that the Guardian is designed to natively drive a wide variety of existing digital and legacy analog autopilots (like older Bendix/King or S-TEC models), meaning the owner doesn't necessarily have to buy a new autopilot to get a new glass panel. *Note: It explicitly does not integrate with Garmin autopilots.*
- **System Interconnects:** The N101000 LRU module acts as the system's primary translator, handling all analog-to-digital conversions for legacy sensors and interfacing the high-speed data buses.

## The Bottom Line

The Nighthawk Guardian is an aggressively innovative system that directly attacks the weight and installation complexity associated with traditional glass cockpits. For the technician, the tool-less NEST architecture and remote-mounting flexibility offer a refreshing departure from standard industry practices.
