# Garmin G3X Touch: Wide-Reaching STC

**Source:** Aviation Consumer
**Author:** Larry Anglisano
**Date:** May 2019
**URL:** https://aviationconsumer.com/aircraftreviews/garmin-g3x-touchwide-reaching-stc/
**Category:** EFIS/PFD / Integrated Flight Deck
**Products covered:** Garmin G3X Touch, GDU470, GDU460, GSU25D, G5, GFC 500, GTN 650, G500/600 TXi, Dynon SkyView Certified

---

Garmin achieved a significant milestone by obtaining an STC for its G3X Touch integrated glass panel across over 500 aircraft models within a single year. The system evolved from the company's experimental aviation division.

## Configuration Options and Pricing

The G3X Touch comes in multiple display configurations: 7-inch portrait display starting at $7,995, 10.6-inch landscape display starting at $9,995, dual 7-inch portrait at $12,865, and combined 10.6-inch and 7-inch setup at $14,865.

Base systems include synthetic vision, Connext wireless technology, FliteCharts, SafeTaxi diagrams, and sectional/IFR charts.

## Engine Instrumentation System

The optional EIS adds $3,000 for four-cylinder engines ($3,300 for six-cylinder). It functions as an approved replacement for factory engine and fuel quantity instruments, integrating with the Crew Alerting System for intuitive alerts.

## Compatibility — Garmin Ecosystem Only

The G3X Touch requires digital Garmin equipment throughout. Compatible units include GNS530W/430W, GTN series navigators, GPS175, GNX375, GTR225 radio, and GTX digital transponders. It cannot interface with third-party analog VHF navigators. In its experimental form, all G3X Touch interfaces were closed loop, digital and based around Garmin navigators.

## Autopilot Integration

The GFC500 autopilot is not included and starts at $6,995. The system can interface with third-party autopilots for basic heading and course command plus GPS steering, but will not display flight director command bars or autopilot modes for non-Garmin systems.

## Backup Systems

The STC permits complete removal of the aircraft's vacuum system. The G5 EFIS display, with battery backup, syncs fully with the G3X Touch. If the primary display fails, the G5 assumes primary flight display duties and continues driving the GFC500 autopilot.

## G3X Touch vs. G500/600 TXi

7-inch G500 TXi: $11,995 (vs. $7,995 G3X Touch). 10.6-inch G500 TXi: $15,995 (vs. $9,995 G3X Touch). The TXi series, a full TSO'd system, accommodates third-party analog and digital radios plus multiple autopilots — offering broader compatibility than the Garmin-only G3X Touch ecosystem.

## Complete System Pricing Example

A test Grumman Tiger with four-display G3X Touch, EIS, G5 backup, GFC500 autopilot, GTN650, ADS-B transponder, and audio panel totaled approximately $35,000 before installation labor.

## Key Takeaway

The G3X Touch represents Garmin's dominant position through lower entry prices and exceptional STC breadth. But buyers with existing third-party avionics should evaluate the G500/600 TXi for enhanced compatibility.
