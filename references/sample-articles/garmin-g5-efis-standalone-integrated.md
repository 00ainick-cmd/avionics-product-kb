# Garmin G5 EFIS: Standalone, Integrated

**Source:** Aviation Consumer
**Author:** Larry Anglisano
**Date:** May 2016 (updated December 2019)
**URL:** https://aviationconsumer.com/avionics/garmin-g5-efis-standalone-integrated/
**Category:** EFIS/PFD / Flight Instruments
**Products covered:** Garmin G5, G3X Touch, GMC307, GSA28 servos, GMU 11

---

Garmin's G5 electronic flight instrument extends beyond its target market of light sport and experimental aircraft. The device fits perfectly into a standard 3-inch attitude instrument cutout, making it suitable as a primary attitude instrument for vintage steam gauge-equipped panels.

## Key Features

The G5 functions as a comprehensive EFIS with multiple capabilities: built-in air data computer, WAAS GPS, and external navigation interface. It also serves as an autopilot control head, has a rechargeable lithium-ion battery providing up to four hours of power, and serves as instrument source backup to the G3X Touch during ADAHRS failure.

## Technical Specifications

Physical dimensions are 3.4 x 3.6 x 3.6 inches (2.1 inches deep without battery). The 3.5-inch QVGA LCD with LED backlighting is readable from wide viewing angles. Controls include a rotary function knob, on/off power button with backlight control, and microSD card slot for updates. Starting price: $1,350 with backup battery.

## Display Features

The G5 presents two main pages: PFD and HSI modes.

PFD page elements include airspeed indicator with color-coded speed ranges and trend vector on the left, attitude display with pitch markings at 2.5-degree intervals in the center, altimeter with rolling number tape and selected altitude bug on the right, and slip/skid indicator and vertical speed tape at the bottom.

The HSI page features a rotating compass card, bearing pointer for raw nav data (VOR/localizer or GPS), ILS VDI glideslope display as green diamond, and GPS vertical course data integration.

## Autopilot Integration

When paired with Garmin's GMC307/GMC305 mode controllers and GSA28 servo actuators, the G5 provides autopilot mode annunciation, flight director command capability, vertical speed mode, and automatic altitude capture. The altitude alerter flashes at 1,000 feet above selected altitude, again at 200 feet, then changes from white to yellow if overshot.

## Networking

The device synchronizes with G3X panels via CAN network, allowing synchronized selected altitude, barometric setting, and magnetic heading across displays when magnetometer is connected.

## Compatible External Navigation

Works with Garmin GTN750/650, GNS530/430, GNC255 and SL30 navcomm, and portable GPS outputting NMEA RS-232 data (Garmin aera series).

## Market Assessment

The G5 could compete effectively with Dynon's STC-approved EFIS-D10A in the uncertified avionics market. A certified version could serve as an affordable backup for retrofit and OEM glass panels, addressing a market currently occupied by Sandia and L-3 at higher price points.
