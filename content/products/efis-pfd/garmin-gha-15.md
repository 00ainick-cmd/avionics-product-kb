# Garmin GHA 15 Height Advisor
**Manufacturer:** Garmin
**Price Range:** Low (Sub-$2,500)

Certified in late 2025, the Garmin GHA 15 is a revolutionary "radar altimeter" designed specifically for the general aviation (GA) and experimental markets. 

Historically, true radar altimeters (which measure exact height above the ground rather than barometric altitude above sea level) were heavy, complex, and cost tens of thousands of dollars, relegating them strictly to turbine aircraft and heavy helicopters. The GHA 15 brings this critical safety technology to single-engine pistons and light twins at a fraction of the cost.

## How It Works

The GHA 15 is a tiny, lightweight, solid-state radar unit mounted on the belly of the aircraft. 

- **AGL vs. MSL:** While a traditional altimeter uses barometric pressure to estimate Height Above Sea Level (MSL), the GHA 15 shoots radio waves at the ground to measure exact Height Above Ground Level (AGL).
- **The Operating Envelope:** The unit is designed to provide highly accurate AGL readouts from 500 feet down to the ground. 
- **Display Integration:** The data is piped directly into compatible Garmin flight displays (like the G3X Touch, TXi series, or G500/G600). The pilot sees their exact AGL altitude displayed next to the traditional barometric altitude tape.

## The Safety Benefits

The GHA 15 is a massive safety enhancement for specific types of flying:

1.  **Night and IMC Landings:** During low-visibility approaches or landings on black-hole runways over water or unlit terrain, the GHA 15 provides exact height-above-touchdown data, preventing Controlled Flight Into Terrain (CFIT).
2.  **Seaplane Utility:** Glassy water landings are notoriously dangerous because depth perception is completely lost. The precise AGL readout gives seaplane pilots critical vertical awareness during the flare.
3.  **Audible Callouts:** The system provides audible height callouts through the audio panel from 300 feet down to 0 (e.g., "ten, nine, eight..."). It also features pilot-configurable Decision Height (DH) warnings triggering a "Minimums, Minimums" alert.

## Installation Realities

For the technician, the GHA 15 requires precise calibration to be effective.

- **Mounting & Clearance:** The unit must be mounted firmly to the bottom of the fuselage, ensuring a clear, unobstructed view of the ground. Garmin specifies a minimum of 16 inches of ground clearance for the sensor itself.
- **Interfacing:** It connects via a 22-pin connector, utilizing a high-speed CAN bus and an RS-232 serial data line. For certified aircraft, it fully integrates with the **Garmin GI 275** multifunction instruments (which currently act as the primary display interface for the certified market), while the experimental market integrates it with the G3X Touch.
- **The "Zero Feet Offset" Calibration:** This is the most critical step for the installer. The technician must physically measure the exact vertical distance between the belly-mounted GHA 15 sensor and the bottom of the aircraft's main landing gear tires. This "Zero Feet Offset" must be perfectly programmed into the software; otherwise, the system will read "zero" before the wheels actually touch the runway, or conversely, read a positive altitude while taxiing.

## The Bottom Line

The GHA 15 is a prime example of high-end corporate technology scaling down to the GA market. It provides a level of situational awareness during the landing phase that was previously unavailable to piston pilots, all wrapped in a simple, affordable, and lightweight package.
