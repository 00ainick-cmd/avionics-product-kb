# PS Engineering PMA 450B Audio Panel

**Manufacturer:** PS Engineering
**Category:** Audio Panels
**Price Range:** $2,500–$3,200 (hardware only; variable installation labor)
**STC Coverage:** Comprehensive AML-STC covering almost all Part 23 piston singles and twins.
**Last Updated:** March 2026

---

## What It Is (The Translation)

The PS Engineering PMA 450B is the industry standard for a premium, specialized aircraft audio panel. It is the central nervous system connecting the pilot's and passengers' headsets to the aircraft's communication radios, navigation beacons, and external media.

- **For the Entry-Level Tech (Non-Pilot):** Think of it as the master audio mixer for the airplane. It's the central box that all the headset plugs, microphones, incoming radio calls from Air Traffic Control, and passenger music (like Bluetooth from a phone) plug into. It mixes all these noises together and figures out who needs to hear what.
- **Why Pilots Want It:** If a pilot flies with a spouse or kids, the automatic background-noise blocking combined with Bluetooth phone and music integration makes the cockpit vastly quieter and more enjoyable for the passengers, while allowing the pilot to isolate their own headset completely to focus on Air Traffic Control.

## How It Works

The PMA 450B utilizes a specialized Digital Signal Processor (DSP) array to manipulate audio signals.

- **IntelliVox (Auto-Squelch):** Legacy audio panels require the pilot to manually adjust a "squelch" knob. Turn it too high, and the microphone cuts off the first word of a sentence. Turn it too low, and the background engine noise constantly blasts into everyone's headsets. The PMA 450B analyzes every incoming sound wave. It instantly identifies the specific frequency of human speech and opens the mic 1/1000th of a second later, perfectly blocking propeller noise while cleanly passing voice traffic. 
- **IntelliAudio (Spatial Audio):** Originally developed for fighter pilots at the Air Force Research Laboratory. If the pilot is monitoring both ATC (Comm 1) and the local airport unicom frequency (Comm 2), the PMA 450B uses phase-shifting to make Comm 1 sound like it is coming from the left window, and Comm 2 sound like it is coming from the right window. The human brain can effortlessly separate the two conversations.
- **Flightmate:** A digital flight recording system that loops the last two minutes of ATC instructions. If the pilot misses a rapid-fire clearance ("Cessna Two Bravo cleared to the Boston VOR via Victor 3..."), they push a single button to replay the transmission.

## Installation Considerations

- **The Plug-and-Play Upgrade:** If the aircraft currently has a legacy Garmin GMA 340 audio panel, the PMA 450B is engineered as a direct "slide-in" replacement. A technician simply removes the Garmin from its metal tray, slides the PS Engineering unit in, and the installation is finished without rewiring a single pin. This saves massive amounts of shop labor.
- **The Ground-Up Install:** If the aircraft has an ancient King KMA 20 or similar 1970s audio panel, installing the PMA 450B is extremely labor-intensive. The shop must strip all the legacy 50-year-old shielded wire out of the airframe, run new wires to every headset jack, and re-pin the massive 44-pin high-density connector behind the panel. This forces the installation cost up by $2,000 to $4,000.

## Objective Performance & Specs

- **Display:** High-contrast OLED text display providing a visual readout of the active intercom mode and paired Bluetooth devices.
- **Bluetooth:** Twin Bluetooth modules allow a pilot to connect a cell phone to retrieve instrument clearances on the ground, while a passenger connects a separate iPad to stream music to the back seats.
- **Intercom Capability:** Supports up to 6 independent cabin seats.
- **USB Charging:** Features a built-in, TSO'd 10-watt USB charging port permanently mounted directly on the faceplate to power an iPad.

## Related Products

- **Garmin GMA 345:** Direct competitor offering auto-squelch, Bluetooth, and audio playback in the same price range.
- **Garmin GMA 350c:** Flagship Garmin audio panel with 3D spatial audio and voice command processing. Higher price point. Often integrated with Garmin GTN 750 touchscreen configurations.

## Key Technical Facts

- Direct slide-in replacement for legacy Garmin GMA 340 audio panels — same mounting tray, no rewiring.
- IntelliVox auto-squelch uses DSP to identify human speech frequencies and reject propeller/engine noise.
- IntelliAudio spatial audio (developed from Air Force Research Laboratory technology) phase-shifts radio channels for left/right ear separation.
- Flightmate digital recording loops last two minutes of ATC audio for instant replay.
- Twin Bluetooth modules support simultaneous phone and media device connections.
- Built-in TSO'd 10-watt USB charging port on the faceplate.
