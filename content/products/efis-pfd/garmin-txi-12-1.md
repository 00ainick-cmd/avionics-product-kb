# Garmin TXi 12.1-Inch Display
**Manufacturer:** Garmin
**Price Range:** $25,000 - $35,000+ (depending on existing sensors and enablement)

Introduced in July 2025, the 12.1-inch TXi display is a massive visual upgrade to Garmin's wildly popular G500/G600 TXi series. Designed to maximize panel real estate in high-end singles, twins, and turboprops, this display offers significantly more screen area without requiring a completely new panel cut compared to the older 10.6-inch model.

For the avionics technician, the 12.1" TXi is the ultimate "wow factor" upgrade to sell to owners who want the largest possible glass in their panel. 

## The "Form Factor" Advantage

The most critical engineering achievement of the 12.1" TXi is its physical footprint. Garmin designed the display to have the **exact same width** as the existing 10.6-inch TXi display. 

- **Vertical Expansion:** It utilizes vertical space that is often wasted or covered by plastic bezels in older aircraft panels. 
- **33% More Screen:** By expanding vertically, Garmin achieved a 33% increase in usable screen area.
- **Easier Retrofit:** Because the width is identical, shops upgrading an aircraft from a 10.6" TXi (or even older G500/G600 systems) don't have to completely redesign the entire lateral layout of the radio stack and co-pilot instruments. It primarily requires vertical clearance adjustments.

## Why the Size Matters

The extra screen real estate isn't just for show; it substantially improves pilot workflow:

1.  **Split-Screen Capabilities:** The primary advantage is the ability to comfortably split the screen into a massive PFD (Primary Flight Display) and a highly usable MFD (Multi-Function Display) simultaneously. 
2.  **Engine Indication System (EIS):** In aircraft utilizing the TXi as a primary engine monitor, the 12.1" screen allows for the EIS strip to be displayed constantly without crowding the PFD or minimizing the moving map.
3.  **Approach Plates:** Viewing full-screen or split-screen instrument approach plates (now augmented by Garmin SmartCharts) is vastly improved, reducing the need to pan or zoom during critical phases of flight.

## Installation Considerations

### Panel Real Estate
While the width is the same as the 10.6", the height is significantly taller. Technicians must carefully measure the vertical space from the glareshield down to the yoke/control column sub-panel to ensure the display fits without interfering with structural members or yoke travel.

### Cooling and Power
Larger screens draw more power and generate more heat. Proper forced-air cooling (often via a dedicated avionics cooling fan or integrated Garmin cooling solutions) is mandatory to ensure longevity and prevent thermal shutdowns.

## Key Technical Facts
- **Upgrade Path:** The 12.1-inch display has the same width as the 10.6-inch TXi, simplifying upgrades from existing 10.6-inch installations. Vertical clearance is the primary installation consideration.
- **Target Applications:** Suited for high-end singles, twins, turboprops, and King Airs where maximum screen real estate is desired. The 33% increase in screen area over the 10.6-inch model accommodates split-screen PFD/MFD and constant EIS strip display.
- **Cooling:** Larger screen requires proper forced-air cooling to prevent thermal shutdowns.
