# Used Avionics Market: More Volatile Than Ever

**Source:** Aviation Consumer
**Author:** Larry Anglisano
**Date:** February 2022
**URL:** https://aviationconsumer.com/accessories/used-avionics-market-more-volatile-than-ever/
**Category:** Shop Business / Market Analysis
**Products covered:** Garmin GNS 530W, GNS 430W, GTN 750, GTN 650, Avidyne IFD440/540, King KCS55A, Aspen Evolution 2000

---

The used avionics market is experiencing unprecedented volatility driven by supply chain disruptions, increased demand, and labor shortages. Prices have skyrocketed while availability has plummeted.

## Supply Chain Crisis

Component shortages severely impact avionics shops and resellers. When owners remove equipment for upgrades, those used systems may not reach the resale market for nine months or longer, creating artificial scarcity and premium pricing.

## Pricing Trends

Popular system prices: Garmin GNS 530W (used) ~$9,000+. GNS 430W (used) ~$7,000. GTN 750 (new) $16,245+ (up ~$1,000 from a year prior). GTN 650 (used) $9,500. Avidyne IFD440/540 have limited availability.

Notable used finds include King KCS55A compass system at $4,295 (eBay) and Aspen Evolution 2000 (two-screen) at $10,150 (Arizona Air Salvage).

## Key Issues

Modern avionics suites are deeply integrated, meaning missing a single component can stall entire projects. Aircraft may sit incomplete while awaiting backorder equipment. Used equipment shelves at major shops are notably bare, with quality equipment typically selling before removal from aircraft.

## Recommendations

Source used equipment through trusted avionics shops rather than independently. Verify all necessary connectors, backplates, and FAA documentation before purchase. Consider factory flat-rate repair pricing when evaluating used systems. Take advantage of available inventory immediately rather than waiting.

Industry groups like Aircraft Avionics Exchange (25,000+ members) and Garmin Avionics Enthusiasts (8,000+ members) facilitate transactions.
