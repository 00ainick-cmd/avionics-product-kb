**Category:** Flight Displays (PFD/MFD/EFIS)

## What It Is (The Translation)

The Aspen Evolution Pro MAX is a highly capable electronic Primary Flight Display (PFD) designed to replace mechanical attitude indicators and horizontal situation indicators (HSIs) in legacy aircraft. 

- **For the Entry-Level Tech (Non-Pilot):** Think of it as a tall, rectangular digital screen that replaces two essential round analog gauges stacked on top of each other. The big selling point for you as an installer is that it's physically shaped to slide perfectly into those two existing holes, saving you from having to cut a completely new metal dashboard.
- **Why Pilots Want It:** It offers the safety and situational awareness of a full "glass cockpit" (including 3D terrain maps and advanced autopilot steering) at a fraction of the installation labor cost of a large-format display like a Garmin G500 TXi or Dynon HDX.

## How It Works

The Aspen Pro MAX utilizes an integral, GPS-aided Air Data Computer (ADC) and Attitude Heading Reference System (AHRS). Unlike mechanical gyros, solid-state sensors determine pitch, roll, and heading. 

The vertical orientation of the screen is deliberate: the top half functions as the Attitude Indicator (displaying synthetic vision, airspeed/altitude tapes), and the bottom half functions as the Electronic HSI (displaying navigation course, glideslope, and bearing pointers).

- **GPS Steering (GPSS):** The unit translates digital roll steering commands from a GPS navigator (like a Garmin GTN or Avidyne IFD) into analog signals that older autopilots can understand, effectively giving legacy autopilots the ability to smoothly fly complex GPS approaches and holding patterns.
- **The "MAX" Upgrade:** The newer "MAX" series features significantly faster processors, brighter screens, vibrant colors, higher reliability, and a larger font size compared to the original legacy Aspen Evolution displays. 

## Installation Considerations

- **The Slide-In Design:** The defining feature of the Aspen is its form factor. The bezel is precisely engineered to slide directly into the existing 3-inch instrument holes left behind by the mechanical AI and HSI. This completely eliminates the costly need to fabricate, cut, and paint a brand new metal instrument panel.
- **Remote Sensor Unit (RSM):** Requires the installation of a Remote Sensor Module on the exterior of the aircraft (usually on the upper fuselage). This pod contains the magnetometer, outside air temperature probe, and emergency GPS receiver. It requires careful routing of wiring from the panel to the mounting location.
- **Autopilot Interface Wizardry:** The Aspen contains an unparalleled analog-to-digital converter suite. It interfaces flawlessly with almost every legacy analog autopilot (BendixKing KFC 200, Century III, S-TEC 55X) without requiring expensive external adapter boxes.
- **Redundancy:** If the pilot installs a secondary Aspen unit (an Evolution MFD1000 MAX Multi-function Display) alongside the primary, it provides complete cross-cockpit redundancy. If the main PFD fails, the pilot presses a single "REV" (Reversionary) button, and the Multi-Function Display instantly becomes a fully functioning primary flight display.

## Objective Performance & Specs

- **Display:** 6.0-inch diagonal TFT Active Matrix LCD (resolution 400x760). High refresh rate.
- **Synthetic Vision:** Included standard. Visualizes terrain, obstacles, and runways in 3D.
- **Internal Battery:** Integrated backup battery provides 30 minutes of emergency power; an optional extended battery provides 2 hours.
- **Upgradability:** Designed so legacy Aspen owners can send their old units back to the factory to be upgraded to the "MAX" internals at a discount.

## Related Products

- **Garmin GI 275:** Also mounts into standard 3-inch holes. Uses two separate round screens (one for attitude, one for heading) rather than a single vertical display.
- **Garmin G500 TXi / Dynon HDX:** Large-format touchscreen systems offering more screen real estate, engine monitoring, and touchscreen interfaces. Higher installed cost due to panel fabrication labor.

## Key Technical Facts

- Slides into existing 3-inch instrument cutouts — no panel cutting required.
- Integral GPS-aided ADAHRS (Air Data Computer + Attitude Heading Reference System) with solid-state sensors.
- Built-in GPS steering (GPSS) converts digital roll steering commands to analog signals for legacy autopilot compatibility.
- Interfaces with BendixKing KFC 200, Century III, S-TEC 55X, and other legacy autopilots without external adapter boxes.
- "MAX" series upgrade features faster processors, brighter screens, and improved reliability over original Evolution displays.
- Internal backup battery provides 30 minutes of emergency power (optional extended battery: 2 hours).
