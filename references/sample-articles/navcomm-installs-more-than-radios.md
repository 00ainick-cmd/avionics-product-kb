# Navcomm Installs: More Than Radios

**Source:** Aviation Consumer
**Author:** Larry Anglisano
**Date:** September 2023
**URL:** https://aviationconsumer.com/avionics/navcomm-installs-more-than-radios/
**Category:** Comm Radios / NavComm
**Products covered:** Garmin GNC 255, Trig TX56/TX57, Honeywell KX 200, Garmin GI 275, King KX155

---

This article examines modern navcomm radio installations, discussing their resurgence as backup systems due to GPS signal outage concerns. Whether for backing up a GPS navigator or serving as a primary and one-and-only radio, the digital navcomm radio is far from extinct.

## Budget Expectations

Budget approximately $6,000 for navcomm upgrades. Buyers seeking navcomm upgrades should also obtain quotes for panel-mounted GPS systems with integral comm radios.

## Featured Equipment

The Garmin GNC 255 ($5,195) is 1.65 inches tall with a 3.46-inch LCD display, standard 10-watt comm transmitter (16-watt option available), and 8.33 kHz frequency spacing. Compatible with King KI209 CDI units via analog output.

The Trig TX56/TX57 ($4,175) is 1.3 inches tall with D-type connectors, built-in intercom with voice-activated operation, and compatible with legacy King CDIs and modern Garmin/Dynon displays.

The Honeywell KX 200 ($5,100) is a drop-in replacement for KX155 with color LCD display, 50-channel memory, and existing KX155 interface wiring compatible.

## Display Considerations

Electronic instruments like Garmin's GI 275 ($3,395) function as electronic HSI or traditional CDI and accept multiple nav sources. The Aspen EFD1000 Evolution accepts analog inputs through an optional external ACU.

## Installation Complexity

The performance of your new rig will only be as good as the wiring and accessories it's connected with. Variables affecting installation costs include existing wiring condition, antenna replacement needs, and CDI compatibility requirements.

## Recommendations

Obtain shop quotes accounting for wiring assessment, antenna evaluation, and potential replacement needs before committing to upgrades.
