# Autopilot Repairs: Parts Obsolescence

**Source:** Aviation Consumer
**Author:** Larry Anglisano
**Date:** March 2025
**URL:** https://aviationconsumer.com/avionics/autopilot-repairs-parts-obsolescence/
**Category:** Autopilots / Shop Business / Repair vs Replace
**Products covered:** BendixKing KAP140, S-TEC 55X, Avidyne DFC90, Garmin GFC 500, Genesys S-TEC 3100, Trio Pro Pilot, KFC200

---

This article examines the challenges aircraft owners face when dealing with aging autopilot systems, emphasizing the difficult choice between costly repairs and complete replacement of obsolete equipment.

## Parts Obsolescence and Service Limitations

Older analog autopilot systems from BendixKing, Century, and S-TEC are increasingly difficult to maintain. Shops without access to service documents or specialized test equipment may blindly swap out boxes. Mid-Continent Instruments stopped working on older Bendix M4C/D servo models due to servo clutch and motor extinction.

## Shortage of Skilled Technicians

The retirement of experienced bench technicians has created a knowledge gap. Newer technicians lack expertise in 50-year-old analog systems while managing modern digital platforms.

## Specific Systems

BendixKing KAP140, once standard in Cessna and Piper aircraft from the late 1990s-early 2000s, now faces significant parts availability challenges.

S-TEC systems are popular in the retrofit market but have limited field service since maintenance manuals were decommissioned. Repairs are typically only available through factory, with servos potentially falling below repairable serial number ranges. Major repairs to an S-TEC 55X run near $4,000.

## Modern Alternatives

The Avidyne DFC90 ($11,350) is a drop-in replacement for S-TEC 55X systems in Cirrus SR20/22 and Piper PA-46 models. Add $4,000 for Mod55 modifications if needed.

Garmin's GFC 500 currently dominates the single-engine retrofit market with continuously expanding STC approvals.

The Genesys S-TEC 3100 is a newer digital option compatible with Garmin and Aspen displays, available for Cessna, Beech, and Piper models.

Trio Pro Pilot is a lower-cost alternative gaining market traction.

## Key Recommendations

Thoroughly test autopilot performance during demo flights before purchasing used aircraft. One buyer discovered pitch oscillations and expensive gyro/servo issues only after closing the deal, requiring over $14,000 in repairs within six months.

Spending nearly $10,000 or more to get an old system working is usually throwing good money after bad. Obtain quotes for new autopilot systems before committing to major repairs on aging equipment.
